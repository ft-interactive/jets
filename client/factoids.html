Nine things you did not know about the world of corporate jet perks

The biggest publicly-listed US companies spent $40m last year giving free flights on the corporate jet to some of their highest-paid executives. The Financial Times analysed more than a thousand securities filings to

The tax implications of letting executives use the corporate airplane for personal travel could sometimes be more costly than the flights themselves. Boston Scientific spent $39,982 providing personal flights to its chief executive Michael Mahoney in 2013, but the medical device maker also said that it lost $152,884 in corporate income tax deductions as a result.
Why does Corning, the glass-making company, provide corporate aircraft perquisites to its executives? Because of Corning, New York. The company's compensation committee explains: The Committee believes that a well-managed program of limited personal aircraft use, given the limited commercial flight options available in the Corning, New York area, provides an extremely important benefit at a reasonable cost to the Company.
Own a private airplane and want to use it at work? You could get reimbursed for that, if you are senior enough. In 2014 Google paid Eric Schmidt $7,500 per hour of business use of Mr Schmidt's planes, for a total payment of $1.3m. Oracle paid a company owned by Larry Ellison, its executive chairman, $1.4m in 2014 using its aircrafts. Likewise, Cisco reimbursed chairman and chief executive John Chambers $2.5m. In each case, the company said it paid less than the market rate. Google, in particular, noted that the reimbursement was less than the cost incurred by Mr Schmidt.
Ralph Lauren was reimbursed $200,000 in both 2013 and 2014 by his eponymous company … for flying on his own private airplane for personal travel.
James Gorman, chairman and chief executive of Morgan Stanley rang up a $237,968 bill for one emergency round trip flight to Australia (presumably from New York, where Morgan Stanley is headquartered). It was due to a death in Mr Gormanâ€™s family, the company explained.
It is not always just free flights on the corporate airplane. CableVision Systems gave $271,761 worth of free flights on the company helicoptor to three executives in 2013. "Personal use of the helicopters has primarily been for purposes of commutation[sic]," the company said.
One railway company, CSX Corporation, provides free flights on the corporate plane to its chief executive.
What is the value of having an executive not take personal flights on the the corporate aircraft? In 2013 Leucadia National, owner of Jeffries investment bank, paid Ian Cummings, its chairman, $600,000 for him to relinquish his right to personal use of the company airplane between June 2013 and his retirement in June 2015.
Barry Diller is chairman of two digital media companies — Expedia and IAC — and has been receiving free personal flights on the corporate jets from both companies.
